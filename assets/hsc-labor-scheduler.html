⚙ Prep Labor Is a Fixed Cost — Treat It That Way

Prep labor (AM bake-off, stock, mise en place, PM setup) happens regardless of how the day performs. On a slow Monday, you still need Louise in at 5am and 1–2 cooks prepping by 7am. That's a floor you can't go below. The scheduler now separates this from service labor so you can see what's truly fixed vs. what flexes with revenue.

⚠ The Service Crew Problem

Your service labor should arrive at 3:30 PM every day — full stop. Your revenue data shows 74% of daily revenue begins at 4 PM. Every service body paid before 3:30 PM is eating margin against a fraction of your revenue. Set a hard rule: service crew = 3:30 PM start, no exceptions except on peak days when a 1pm PM prep team is needed.

✓ The One-Cook Transition Rule (Mon–Wed)

On slow days, 1 cook can carry breakfast all the way through lunch and into PM prep without a shift change. Louise covers bake-off + pastry from 5am. One line cook transitions breakfast → lunch → starts dinner prep by 1pm. Second BOH arrives at 3:30 for service. That's your entire BOH on a $2,200 day — and it lands under budget.

📈 Sunday Is Moving Up

Sunday is trending above Thursday consistently at $4,100+ average. If this holds through summer, you have a 4-day weekend. Watch 8 consecutive Sundays. When Sunday clears $4,500 consistently, promote it to Tier 3 staffing.
