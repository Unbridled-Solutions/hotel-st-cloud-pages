⚠ The Breakfast Problem

Breakfast (7–11 AM) generates only 12% of daily revenue. On a Monday generating $2,200, that's roughly $264 in the morning window. You cannot staff a full BOH crew to generate $264. One cook + Louise for bake-off covers breakfast and transitions straight through to lunch without a shift change.

⚠ The Lunch Gap (Mon–Wed)

Lunch (12–3 PM) on slow days adds ~$300 in revenue. You're paying for a full changeover to generate less than a typical dinner ticket. Recommendation: condensed bar-bites menu at Fremont Public House only, close 1887 for lunch Mon–Wed, and let the breakfast cook hold through to dinner prep.

✓ The 3:30 PM Rule

Dinner crew should arrive at 3:30 PM, not 11 AM or 2 PM. Your data shows 74% of revenue begins at 4 PM. Every BOH dollar spent before 3:30 PM on a slow day is paid against a fraction of the revenue. Set the standard: dinner crew = 3:30 PM start, regardless of day.

📈 Sunday Is Moving Up

Sunday is now tracking above Thursday revenue consistently at $4,100+ average. If this trend holds through summer, you have a 4-day weekend (Thu–Sun), not a 2-day one. Watch the next 8 Sundays. If Sunday consistently clears $4,500, promote it to Tier 3 staffing.

✓ Cross-Train FOH Across All Three Venues

1887, Fremont Public House, and The War Room should be staffed by a flexible FOH team, not three dedicated crews. One server who opens 1887 transitions to the Fremont bar at 11 AM and then supports War Room at 5 PM. With 36 keys driving F&B, you can't afford three siloed teams on weekdays.
