The Opportunity
Why Douglas County. Why Now.

Douglas County is one of the wealthiest, most active counties in Colorado — Parker, Castle Rock, Highlands Ranch, Lone Tree. Median household income over $120K. A population that runs, bikes, lifts, and invests heavily in health optimization. They already buy supplements, wear CGMs, and book functional medicine appointments.

The peptide market is in a perfect growth window: consumer awareness is spiking off the back of GLP-1 and semaglutide coverage, biohacking culture has gone mainstream, and the regulatory gray zone is starting to clarify as clinic-based models gain legitimacy. A credible, Colorado-rooted brand with a clean aesthetic and science-first positioning can own this market locally — and scale nationally from here.

The global peptide therapeutics market is projected at $164 billion in 2026, growing to $295 billion by 2033. DTC peptide cosmetics alone hit $34.6B in 2025. The window is open. The question is who claims the high ground first.

The positioning gap: Most DTC peptide brands look like either gray-market research chemical shops or cold clinical pharma. Neither owns the premium wellness space — the Apple-meets-Colorado-outdoors aesthetic nobody has built yet. That's the white space.

Campaign Strategy
How to Go to Market

Google/Meta will restrict peptide advertising if you lead with therapeutic claims. The strategy is built around this reality: lead with identity and lifestyle, funnel to consultation or purchase, and let the landing page do the medical/scientific heavy lifting.

🔍
Google Search — Intent Capture
Capture high-intent searches without triggering pharmacy policies. Target wellness and lifestyle terms. Keywords: "peptide therapy Colorado," "biohacking Douglas County," "performance optimization Parker CO," "anti-aging clinic Castle Rock," "hormonal optimization Colorado." Avoid: injection, dosage, cure, treat.
📸
Meta / Instagram — Audience Building
Lead generation and brand awareness via lookalike audience targeting. Seed audience: Huberman Lab listeners, Peter Attia followers, CrossFit/functional fitness, functional medicine interest. Use lifestyle visuals — summit, trail runner. No product claims, no injection imagery.
📧
Email / SMS Nurture
The ad drives to a consult or free protocol guide opt-in. Email sequence of 5–7 touches: education first (what peptides are), then social proof, then offer. Average protocol value $449–$1,295/cycle — a well-built email funnel pays off fast.
🤝
Local Partnerships
Douglas County is dense with the target customer. Partner channels: functional medicine clinics, CrossFit boxes, boutique fitness studios, sport performance coaches in Parker/Castle Rock/Highlands Ranch. Referral program beats any CPM.
🧠
Content / SEO Foundation
Long game. Peptide education content ranks well because most competitors are thin on substance. Content angles: "What is BPC-157?", "Peptides vs. HGH," "How to start a peptide protocol," "Colorado functional wellness guide." Blog + YouTube.
⚠️
Compliance First
Before any ad goes live: legal review of all copy for FDA compliance. All claims reviewed against FTC guidelines. Safe framing: "wellness," "optimization," "performance support" — not "treats," "cures," "diagnoses." Consult-first model provides the cleanest regulatory path.
Recommended model: Clinic-supervised or consult-first DTC. It's the cleanest path through Google/Meta policy, the most defensible from an FDA perspective, and it commands the premium price ($449–$1,295/cycle) that makes the unit economics work.

Launch Timeline
90-Day Playbook

Three phases: build the foundation, light the match, then scale what's working. Nothing goes live until it's legally clean and the funnel is built end-to-end.

1
Weeks 1–2 · Foundation
Brand, Legal & Infrastructure
  • Finalize company name, logo, and color system
  • Legal review of all claims — FDA compliance pass on every word
  • Website domain + hosting setup
  • Landing page built: hero → opt-in → consult booking
  • CRM + email sequence wired (5–7 touch nurture flow)
  • Google Ads and Meta ad accounts created and verified
2
Weeks 3–4 · Soft Launch
First Traffic & Local Partnerships
  • Google Search campaigns live — 3 ad groups, $50–75/day, performance + longevity + biohacking
  • Meta awareness campaign live — brand video + summit image, $25/day
  • Reach out to 5–10 Douglas County functional medicine clinics, CrossFit boxes
  • First content piece published: "What Are Peptides & Why Colorado Athletes Use Them"
  • Set up conversion tracking — form fills, consult bookings, phone calls
3
Weeks 5–8 · Optimization
Read the Data, Cut What Doesn't Work
  • First performance review — which ad groups are generating consult bookings?
  • Add negative keywords from search term report
  • OPtimize landing page based on heatmap/scroll data
  • Scale budget 20–30% on top performers
  • Publish 2–3 more content pieces targeting Colorado + peptide search intent
  • First referral program launch — partner clinic incentive
4
Weeks 9–12 · Scale
Find the Unit Economics, Then Push
  • Know your cost-per-consult and consult-to-client conversion rate
  • If CPL is under $150 and conversion rate above 20% → aggressively scale ad spend
  • Expand geographic targeting to Denver Metro / Front Range
  • Launch retargeting campaigns — website visitors who didn't book
  • Begin building email list for long-term SEO + authority plays
  • Month 3 goal: 20+ active clients, $10K+ MRR, referral engine running
Before anything goes live: An attorney with FTC/FDA experience needs to review all copy, claims, and product descriptions. This brief is a creative and strategic starting point — not legal clearance. One disapproved ad or one FDA warning letter costs more than the legal review. Do it first.
