MC's Take

Royal Gorge Basecamp is the efficiency story of the week — nearly 6 conversions on $342 spend puts its cost-per-conversion well under $60, and it's doing that while maintaining a 7.75% CTR from under 2,000 impressions. That's a focused, high-intent audience responding. The Memorial Day weekend spike on May 27 (3 conversions in one day) suggests seasonal demand is real and worth pushing harder into summer.

Local Proximity is the volume leader but burning the most budget at $508 — it's grabbing that single conversion on its highest-spend day (May 29). The 19.77% CTR is legitimately impressive and tells you the ads are connecting with the right people, but with only 1 tracked conversion across 7 days, the landing page or attribution chain deserves a close look. This campaign has enough click intent to warrant conversion rate optimization before scaling spend further.

Fremont Provisions is spending $227 with zero conversions — the impressions and clicks are consistent (189 clicks across 7 days is not nothing), but no tracked action is a red flag. If the conversion goal is foot traffic or phone calls rather than form fills, confirm the conversion tracking is properly configured. Without it, we're flying blind on ROI for the sports bar. Standard Oil Coffee is running on minimal budget and showing it; worth treating as a test-and-learn campaign until the tracking story is cleaner.
