Weekly Analysis

Local Proximity & Through Traffic is the clear standout this week — a 20.84% CTR is exceptional for any paid search campaign, and pulling in 5 conversions at a $108/conversion cost-per is exactly the kind of highway intercept performance we want. That's the campaign driving real heads-in-beds results. Watch it closely and don't let budget cap it mid-week.

FP Sports Bar leads in raw impressions (4,158) and ties for most clicks (214), but with zero conversions, the volume is buying awareness, not bookings. If game-day traffic is the goal, that's fine — but if this is meant to drive reservations or visits, the landing experience needs a look. The CTR is modest at 5.15%, suggesting the ad copy and audience targeting may have room to sharpen.

Royal Gorge Basecamp is the quietest of the hotel campaigns — solid CTR at 8.58%, pacing on spend alongside FP Sports Bar, but showing a June 4th spike in both clicks and cost that didn't repeat. Worth checking if that was a promotion, a competitor pulling back, or just day-of-week variance. Zero conversions here needs to be addressed if this campaign is running intent-based keywords.

Bars and Bathrooms and Standard Oil Coffee are both early-stage — B&B only has one day of real data in this window, and Coffee is erratic (two zero-click days). Give them another week before drawing conclusions, but Standard Oil's low CTR at 2.74% suggests the creative or targeting isn't connecting yet. It's a great concept; the ads just need to find their audience.
