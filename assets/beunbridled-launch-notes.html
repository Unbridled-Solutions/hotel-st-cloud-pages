BeUnbridled Launch — Meeting Notes

June 4, 2026 Stan Bullis · Tim Woodring Confidential

About this document: Summary of the Stan Bullis and Tim Woodring strategy session on launching BeUnbridled AI. Covers the product vision, three-hat framework, org structure debate, cap table structure, talent pipeline, and open action items. Compiled by MC from the live recording.

Product Vision

Three Distinct Products — Not One

Product 1

BeUnbridled

Digital Chief of Staff for solopreneurs, small businesses, restaurants, churches, and blue collar businesses. Off-grid, fast to deploy, recurring revenue. Most marketable.

Product 2

Unbridled Internal

Personal AI for each team member (Sabrina's email, document drafts, responses). Hub-building, SaaS replacement, data plumbing. Hardest — requires change management, Microsoft tenant navigation, slow by nature.

Product 3

Sol4 / Enterprise

AI setup + infrastructure as part of Sol4's MSP offering to enterprise customers. Needs proactive solutioning, not just setup and management. Stan's role: advisory + direction.

Stan's Framework

The Three Hats Stan Sees AI Through

  • Entrepreneurial Hat (BeUnbridled): Cap table partner, build something specialized, defensible, recurring revenue. The fun work. Stan sees this as the most important thing to focus on right now.
  • Unbridled Hat: AI adoption can't live in IT alone — it must live in operators, architects, solution-builders. Stan's role: advocate and unlocker, not gatekeeper or bottleneck. Chris Howell alignment: decentralize AI to the people doing the work.
  • Sol4 Hat: Executive sponsor alongside Kerry (Stan = software direction, Kerry = fiscal). Pushing Sol4 to be solutions-oriented and entrepreneurial about AI — not just managing infrastructure. Going to Paxate conference (Salt Lake City, Mon–Tue) to learn the MSP/AI market.
"I think my time's going to free up. And I can run — we don't need a lot to support the baseline of this. We need 15 people at $1,000 a month to cover a gesture." — Stan

BeUnbridled Revenue Model

"When you double down on who you are and what you do for people, that perpetuates and accelerates. Say no to things that look more enterprise once you start seeing security and compliance creep in." — Stan
Key Tension

Inside vs. Outside — The Debate

⟷ The Core Tension

  • Tim's view: Unbridled needs a dedicated maker inside the org — someone fully focused on building the hub, not fractionalized. Justin is the model. If he were 100% on this, inside Solutions, that's what it looks like.
  • Stan's view: Keep BeUnbridled as the incubator. Bill back to Unbridled on retainer. IP and learning compounds in an incubator that benefits the whole ecosystem. Solutions has tried to build internally (apps, gamification, web dev) and nothing stuck. "We kill everything."
  • Where it landed: Agree to disagree, but Stan's direction is to pull Justin and possibly Brandon into BeUnbridled as a shared service model. Solutions gets a dedicated resource eventually — it comes from BeUnbridled headcount, not a Solutions hire.
"Justin called me yesterday. He's like, 'I'm getting pulled in a hundred different directions every single day.' He's talking about taking PTO just so he can focus on building." — Stan

💡 Tim's Concession

  • Tim accepts the BeUnbridled incubator model — with conditions: clear access points to Unbridled, dedicated focus, and willingness to pivot if needed
  • "If BeUnbridled runs in the direction of solopreneurs and Unbridled is a different thing altogether — let's hire a dedicated person inside and treat bigger pushes as special projects."
  • The flexibility to pivot and change direction is important — we don't know everything on the front end
