Scott —

We have been working together for over twenty years — all the way back to when you were sixteen years old. In that time, our relationship has twisted and turned and elaborated — great moments, hard moments, a full life cycle shared between two people who have been doing this long enough to know that the good ones are rare.

I did not take this opportunity lightly. When you gave me the challenge of clarifying your role as Chief Commercial Officer, I took it seriously. What follows is the result of two full weeks of work — my best thinking, my heart, my vision for Unbridled and for you. It has my voice. It has my heart. And it is strategically designed around where I see the future of this ecosystem going.

My sincere hope is that twenty-five years from now, we are still working together. I literally, figuratively, and emotionally believe that is possible. And so I want you to read what follows not as a performance review — but as an invitation. An honest one. The kind that can feel uncomfortable before it feels clarifying.

"We are building something extraordinary and it's genuinely hard to contain. I think it's about to blow up — in all the best ways. And I want you in the driver's seat when it does."

This is not locked in stone. It's my best thinking. If something doesn't land right, we talk. But I believe this gives you clarity. I believe it highlights your role in the organizational structure — specifically in live events, but not limited to it. If you read it the right way, it should be liberating.

✦
The Ecosystem You're Leading Into

We are building thirty-two companies on a single set of principles. Hub and spoke. Shared services at the center — finance, HR, facilities, marketing, technology, and one consolidated global sales function — with autonomous, equity-holding presidents running each brand. No duplicated overhead. No reporting chains that slow things down. Decisions made closest to the work, by the people who know it best.

In that model, the Chief Commercial Officer is not a manager. You are the person who makes six world-class presidents choose to follow you — not because you hold authority over them, but because you've earned the right to lead them. That right is called relational equity. It is built through proximity, through presence, through investing in relationships before you need them. Through a vision of the future so compelling that presidents feel it would be a mistake to build it without you in the room.

What the Ecosystem Needs from Its CCO
1

A client advocate, not a deal closer. The most powerful thing you can do for commercial growth is make clients feel so seen, so served, and so valued that they never consider leaving — and they bring others with them. That is a relationship job, not a transaction job.

2

A vision carrier, not a manager. Presidents need to be inspired by where this ecosystem is going — and you need to be the one who paints that picture with enough clarity and conviction that they choose to co-create it with you. That requires you to be further forward than anyone else in the room.

3

A trust builder, not an authority holder. In this model, control is a ceiling. The presidents who work alongside you hold equity. They have options. They stay and give everything because they believe in the mission — and because they believe in you. That belief is not granted by a title. It is earned, slowly, through consistent character.

✦
Your Role — Number Two in Live Events

I want to be direct about how I see us: my role is to create inspiration, vision, and direction. Your role is to translate that into the presence of the live events companies. Stan sets the compass. Scott moves the compass through six presidents and makes it real on the ground.

One of the missing pieces is that we still don't have a President of Unbridled Solutions. I believe that is one of our first key priorities — finding that person. Once we do, your role becomes even cleaner: you will have oversight of six or seven live events presidents, inspiring them to take new territory, conquer new ground, and grow into leaders who build other leaders. That is the seat I see you in. And I believe you will thrive there.

The live events vertical is about to take a lot of ground. I believe that. And the question is not whether it will happen — it is whether we are ready to move when the window opens.

✦
The Car Analogy — Why Now Is the Moment

Everyone in our industry is talking about the influence of AI. Most are watching it from the back seat, waiting to see where it goes. A few are in the passenger seat — curious, engaged, offering directions. But very few are actually driving.

Here's the distinction that matters: AI is a tool most people use, but very few people operate. Using it means asking it a question and taking the answer. Operating it means understanding what it can do, building systems around it, and putting it to work in ways your competitors haven't imagined yet. The difference between using a car and driving one isn't the vehicle — it's who controls where it goes.

The car is already moving. The question is not whether AI will reshape live events — it will. The question is who is behind the wheel when it does. Some companies will spend the next three years figuring out how to get in. We are already in the driver's seat. The goal now is to put it in gear, drive with intention, and take ground that others are still standing on the curb debating. In live events, we have a window right now to stop taking a back seat to the industry and use AI to herald our message, accelerate our reach, and serve clients at a level no competitor can match without the infrastructure we've already built.

— Stan Bullis

One of my goals right now is to get you activated and moving — not just observing the transformation but leading it inside the live events vertical. I believe a lot of what we struggle with in leadership — authority, accountability, alignment — we can now address faster and more precisely than ever before. By asking better questions. By using AI to surface what's working and what isn't. By making our always and never visible in our communication and our culture.

✦
The Shift — Leading Through Inspiration

The hard truth: leading through relational equity is a complicated thing. It is deep work. It is not a technique. It requires you to let go of some tools that have worked for you for twenty years — and pick up ones that feel less immediate, less measurable, but ultimately far more powerful.

Satya Nadella had to do this at Microsoft. He described it as moving from a place of "I know it all" to "I want to learn it all." The tools that got you here are not the tools that will take this ecosystem where it needs to go. That's not a diminishment — it's the highest compliment. We believe you can make that shift. The next chapter requires it.

"The ceiling of a leader who builds leaders is generational. But leaders only build leaders when they're genuinely invested in each other. That investment starts with presence, deepens through vulnerability, and compounds through trust. That is the excursion ahead of us. And I believe you will thrive in it."

I also want us to revisit our always and never together — the guardrails that define how we treat each other, how we show up in conflict, how we repair when something breaks. Writing those down and living by them is not a corporate exercise. It is the foundation of a relationship that can go another twenty-five years.

This is an important milestone. I bless you. And I'll see you on the other side of it.
