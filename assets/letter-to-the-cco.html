Twenty years is not a small thing. It means you were here before most of this existed. You helped build the commercial engine that made everything else possible. That contribution is real, it is recognized, and it will always matter.

And because it matters — because you matter — this letter exists. What follows isn't a performance review. It's an invitation. But it's an honest one, and honest invitations sometimes feel uncomfortable before they feel clarifying.

"The ecosystem we are building requires a different kind of leadership at the top. Not lesser — different. And in many ways, harder. Because it asks you to trade a tool you're very good at — control — for one that takes longer to build and is less immediately visible: trust."

The CCO role in this model is not the role you may have been operating in. It is not about managing Presidents toward your preferred outcomes. It is not about being the smartest person in the commercial conversation. And it is not a stepping stone to something else.

It is about becoming the person six world-class leaders choose to follow — not because you have authority over them, but because you've earned the right to lead them. That right is earned through relational equity. Through investing in relationships before you need them. Through a vision of the future so compelling that Presidents feel it would be a mistake to build it without you in the room.

What the ecosystem needs from its CCO
1

A client advocate, not a deal closer. The most powerful thing you can do for commercial growth is make clients feel so seen, so served, and so valued that they never consider leaving — and they bring others with them. That is a relationship job, not a transaction job.

2

A vision carrier, not a manager. Presidents need to be inspired by where this ecosystem is going — and you need to be the one who paints that picture with enough clarity and conviction that they choose to co-create it with you. That requires you to be further forward than anyone else in the room.

3

A trust builder, not an authority holder. In this model, control is a ceiling. The Presidents who report to you hold equity. They have options. They stay and they give everything because they believe in the mission and they believe in you. That belief is not granted by a title. It is earned, slowly, through consistent character.

The hard truth is this: the commercial fundamentals you bring are genuinely valuable. But they are not what will define whether this role — and this season — is everything it could be. What will define it is whether you can make the shift from leading through authority to leading through inspiration. From managing toward outcomes to enrolling people in a future worth running toward.

That shift is not a small ask. It will require you to let go of some things that have worked for you for 20 years. It will feel vulnerable. It will mean having conversations that are relational before they are tactical. It will mean being genuinely curious about what your Presidents need from you — not as a management technique, but as a real expression of care.

Satya Nadella had to do this at Microsoft. He described it as moving from a place of "I know it all" to "I want to learn it all." It wasn't weakness. It unlocked a $3 trillion company. The tools that got you here are not the tools that will take this ecosystem where it needs to go.

The next 90 days will tell the story. Not in the numbers — those will follow. In the relationships. In whether Presidents feel more supported, more inspired, and more connected to a future they want to help build. In whether clients feel genuinely championed at the highest level of the organization. In whether the ecosystem feels like it's accelerating — or just running.

You have built something real over 20 years. The question this moment is asking is whether you're willing to build something new — inside yourself — so that everything you've worked for can become everything it's capable of being. That's not a threat. It's the highest compliment this company can pay you. We believe you can do it. The next move is yours.
