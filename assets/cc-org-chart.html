MC's Assessment — Ecosystem Alignment

Does this structure support the Ecosystems over Egosystems model?

✓ Where it supports the ecosystem model

  • Hub + Spoke + Wheel in action. Allison as SVP is the hub. The four branches (Paul, Danielle, Abbie, Ty) are the spokes. Each spoke's team is a wheel — autonomous, specialized, expandable. This is exactly the model from the culture deck.
  • Authority is distributed. Three Creative Directors each own their own team with full creative accountability. No single person is the bottleneck for creative output. The people closest to the work have the decision rights.
  • Ops is separated from creative. Danielle Miller's VP Ops branch is a smart structural move — it protects creative flow from administrative overhead. Credibility cornerstone at work.
  • New roles are purposeful, not hierarchical. Brandon Emery → Video CD, CJ → Technical Design, Kate → Ops Support. These expand capability without adding management layers. That's ecosystem thinking.
  • Succession is visible. The Graphic Designer who trains with CJ before moving under a CD is a career path, not just a hire. That's "Unusually Good Careers" from the brand strategy.

△ Where it could be strengthened

  • 65% reporting changes is high disruption. 15 of 23 people changing who they report to in one reorg is a significant cultural moment. The comms plan is critical — this needs to feel like an upgrade, not a reshuffling. The Hero's Journey framing from the culture deck is the right lens here.
  • Jil Cave appears in two branches. She's listed under both Danielle Miller/CJ Nuzzo and Ty Stark (until 8/1). That's a split reporting situation that needs a clean transition plan communicated clearly to her.
  • The intern sits outside the structure. No clear reporting line shown. In an ecosystem model, even the intern should know their spoke and their wheel. Small detail, real impact on belonging.
  • Three open Presentation Designer roles. That's a concentration of risk in one discipline. If the team is taking on new work before these are filled, capacity planning needs to account for it explicitly.
  • Tim Woodring as CSO at the top. Healthy that leadership visibility is here, but make sure the SVP has genuine autonomous decision authority — the ecosystem breaks down if Tim has to approve routine creative calls.

Verdict

  • This structure does support the ecosystem model — it distributes authority, creates clear spokes, and builds in career progression. The reorg is moving in the right direction. The risk isn't the structure. It's the transition — 15 people changing managers is a human moment that needs as much attention as the org chart itself. Get the comms plan right and this lands well.
