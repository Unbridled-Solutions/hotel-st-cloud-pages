The Setting

About the Region

Cañon City is one of Colorado’s best-kept secrets — and that’s changing fast. Positioned at the gateway to the Royal Gorge region, it sits at the intersection of adventure, history, and authentic Colorado life. Think Moab before it got discovered. The Arkansas River runs right through town. The Rockies rise in every direction. And Hotel St. Cloud is the only four-star address in the zip code. This is a place that draws adventurers, road trippers, anglers, climbers, and families looking for something real. The kitchen here feeds that guest — and the chef who leads it sets the standard for what this destination can become.

  • Royal Gorge Bridge & Park — one of the world’s highest suspension bridges, drawing 300,000+ visitors annually
  • Gold medal fly fishing on the Arkansas River — some of the best wild trout water in the country
  • World-class rock climbing and bouldering at Shelf Road and the iconic limestone canyons surrounding Cañon City
  • Mountain biking, trail running, and hiking across hundreds of miles of rugged high-desert terrain
  • Whitewater kayaking and rafting on the Arkansas River — Class II floats to Class V rapids
  • Historic Main Street culture, local dining, craft breweries, and a community that punches way above its weight

The Role

Position Summary

We are seeking an exceptional culinary leader to serve as Chef of Hotel St. Cloud. This is not a maintenance role — it’s a builder role. You will own the full culinary identity of our property and lead kitchen operations across all four of our distinct food and beverage outlets. The food here has a voice: Frontier New American — bold, regionally rooted, crafted from scratch, built for the adventurous guest who expects quality without pretension. Your job is to make that voice unmistakable.

Responsibilities

What You’ll Own

Culinary Identity & Menus

Set and evolve the culinary direction across all four outlets — Fremont Public House, 1887 Historic Eatery, The War Room, and catering. Honor each concept’s personality while building a menu philosophy that makes Hotel St. Cloud a culinary destination.

Kitchen Operations

Run a tight, well-trained kitchen. Prep discipline, line execution, service consistency, food safety — all of it lives with you. You set the standard, you enforce it, and you build a BOH culture people are proud to work in.

Team Development

Hire, train, and develop your kitchen team. Teach technique, instill discipline, and mentor the next person down the line. We promote from within when we can — that only works if the chef above them is investing.

Financial Management

Own food cost, labor cost, and waste. Read a P&L, diagnose a margin problem, and adjust without waiting to be told. Build purchasing relationships and make sourcing decisions that keep quality high and costs defensible.

Catering & Events

Hotel St. Cloud is an events property. On-site private dining, off-site catering, and group events are a meaningful revenue stream. Lead kitchen execution for all of it — intimate dinners to large-scale functions — with the same standard you hold on a Saturday dinner service.

Qualifications

What We’re Looking For

  • Culinary credentials from an accredited U.S. culinary school (CIA, Johnson & Wales, Le Cordon Bleu, or equivalent) — you understand the fundamentals at a level that lets you teach them.
  • Proven leadership experience at a recognized boutique hotel, independent restaurant, or resort property — you’ve led a kitchen, not just worked in one.
  • Deep fluency with food cost and labor management: you track your numbers daily, you know your par levels, and you can explain a variance without being asked.
  • Strong command of Frontier New American cuisine — regionally sourced, scratch-prepared, seasonally driven. You’ve built menus with a point of view, not just a price point.
  • Multi-outlet experience — you can hold quality across more than one concept simultaneously without the wheels coming off on a busy Friday.
  • Catering and events experience — you’ve executed for groups, you understand the logistics, and you don’t treat banquets like an afterthought.
  • A people-first leadership style: you build kitchens where people grow, not just kitchens that produce food. Culture is a competitive advantage.
  • The kind of professionalism that travels — you represent Hotel St. Cloud in vendor meetings, community events, and guest interactions with the same polish you bring to a tasting.

What Makes This Role Unique

This isn’t a corporate kitchen role. There’s no regional VP with a standardized menu telling you what to cook. You have real creative authority, a property that is actively becoming a culinary destination, and an ownership group that understands hospitality at a deep level. The Unbridled ecosystem is built on the belief that “Doing Good by Doing Well” isn’t a tagline — it’s an operating model. We invest in our people, we protect the culture, and we build things that last. The right chef will find this to be the most purposeful kitchen they have ever led.

Compensation

Competitive salary commensurate with experience. Benefits package included. Full-time, salaried leadership position.
