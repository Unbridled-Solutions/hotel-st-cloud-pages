About Hotel St. Cloud
The Property

Hotel St. Cloud is Cañon City's only 4-star boutique property — a 36-room landmark at 631 Main Street dedicated to restoring people and places. The hotel is part of the Unbridled ecosystem, a Colorado-based hospitality and events company built on the belief that "Doing Good by Doing Well" is not a tradeoff — it's a strategy.

The property blends historic character with elevated service, anchored by a culinary identity built around Frontier New American cuisine — bold, regionally rooted, scratch-made, and built for the guest who expects quality without pretension.

The Region — Why It Matters

Cañon City sits at the gateway to the Royal Gorge region — one of Colorado's fastest-rising destinations. Think Moab before it got discovered. The Arkansas River runs through town. The Rockies rise in every direction. Hotel St. Cloud is the only four-star address in the zip code.

Key regional draws include: Royal Gorge Bridge & Park (300,000+ visitors/year) · Gold medal fly fishing · World-class rock climbing at Shelf Road · Mountain biking and trail running · Whitewater kayaking and rafting · Historic Main Street culture and craft breweries

What This Person Will Own
Hotel Operations (General Manager)

Full oversight of boutique hotel operations — guest experience, rooms, staff, standards, vendor relationships, and the overall 4-star guest journey. This person sets the culture on property and ensures every interaction reflects Unbridled's commitment to unreasonable hospitality.

Food & Beverage (F&B Director)

Complete ownership of the culinary identity and operational execution across all four outlets and catering. This includes menu development, kitchen culture, front-of-house service standards, vendor and sourcing relationships, and P&L accountability across every concept.

Fremont Public House
Flagship bar and kitchen. Community anchor, full scratch kitchen.
1887 Historic Eatery
Full-service dining with historic character and elevated service.
The War Room
Craft cocktail lounge. Intimate, curated, character-driven.
On- & Off-Site Catering
Private dinners to large-scale events. A significant revenue stream.

Candidate Requirements
Must Have
  • Culinary credentials from an accredited U.S. culinary school (CIA, Johnson & Wales, Le Cordon Bleu, or equivalent)
  • Proven F&B leadership at a recognized boutique or independent hotel — built programs, not just maintained them
  • Deep P&L fluency: diagnose margin leaks, troubleshoot cost variances in real time, build financial discipline without killing culture
  • Experience leading a multi-outlet F&B operation simultaneously (scratch kitchen + craft cocktail program + catering)
  • Track record in boutique or luxury hotel management, ideally in a destination or resort market
  • People-first leadership style: develops team, protects culture, operates with radical generosity
  • Comprehensive on- and off-site catering experience — intimate private dinners to large-scale events
Strong Preference
  • Degree in Hotel Management or Hospitality Administration from an accredited program
  • Fluency with Frontier New American cuisine and regional sourcing philosophy
  • Menu engineering experience — building menus that drive both guest experience AND revenue
  • Experience in a destination/adventure-driven market where hospitality IS the product
  • Comfort operating in a smaller property with a lean team (not a mega-resort operator)
  • Entrepreneurial instincts — this role has room to build, not just execute
🚫
What Will Not Work
  • F&B background limited to chain/branded hotel environment — institutional execution is not the culture here
  • Candidates who want to run the hotel OR the F&B — this is a combined role, not a split one
  • No culinary foundation — the F&B Director must understand the kitchen from the inside out, not just the dining room
  • Command-and-control management style — we develop leaders, not dependents
  • Candidates seeking a large urban property or luxury resort with deep administrative support — this is a lean, entrepreneurial environment
  • Purely financial operators who don't connect with the guest experience side of the role

Culture & Mission Fit
Unbridled Hospitality Group

Doing Good by Doing Well.

Hotel St. Cloud is part of the Unbridled ecosystem — a family of companies built on the belief that business is one of the most powerful forces for good in a community. Profit and purpose are not a tradeoff here. Our 20/20/60 model puts 20% toward philanthropy, 20% back into the business, and 60% to the people and partners who drive the work.

The person who thrives in this role leads with radical generosity — toward guests, toward the team, toward the community. They understand that unreasonable hospitality is not a cost center. It is the product.

What "Fit" Looks Like in Practice

This leader believes that the team member experience drives the guest experience — not the other way around. They protect culture even when it's inconvenient. They build people up rather than create dependency. And they see a boutique property in a rising destination market as an opportunity, not a compromise.

They're not here to punch a ticket. They're here to build something that lasts.
