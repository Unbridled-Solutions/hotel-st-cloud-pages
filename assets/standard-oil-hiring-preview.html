Now Hiring Baristas: The Dual-Purpose Ad Strategy

Two approaches: recruit great baristas while marketing the coffee shop · For Stan's review
MC's Take — The Strategy

The hiring angle is a smart double play. When a specialty coffee shop signals it's looking for qualified baristas, it tells every coffee drinker who sees the ad: we take the craft seriously. It's not just a job posting — it's a credibility signal.

There are two ways to run this. Option A keeps the existing customer-focused ads exactly as approved and adds a hiring callout extension — a small green badge line that says "Now Hiring Baristas" visible on mobile. Zero disruption to the current campaign. Option B builds a dedicated second ad group targeting people searching for barista jobs in Cañon City — it intercepts job seekers and turns them into aware-of-the-brand applicants.

💡 Recommendation: Run both. Option A costs nothing extra — it layers onto the existing $35/day. Option B adds a small separate budget ($10–15/day) and targets a completely different keyword set. Together they cover customers AND recruits without cannibalizing each other.
Option A — Callout Extension on Existing Ads (zero extra budget)
The existing customer-facing ads stay exactly as approved. We add a "Callout Extension" — a small line of text that appears below the ad on mobile. It reads as a natural aside, not a job listing. Google shows it when there's ad space available.
