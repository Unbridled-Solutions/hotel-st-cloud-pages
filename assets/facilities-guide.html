🚨 Stop — When to Call 911 First

🔥 Fire, smoke, or burning smell
Evacuate immediately → CALL 911 → then notify manager
💨 Gas smell anywhere in the building
Do NOT flip switches → Evacuate → CALL 911 → call Atmos Energy: 888-286-6700
⚡ Electrical sparks, burning wires, or shock hazard
Do NOT touch the panel → CALL 911 → notify manager
🏗️ Person trapped in elevator
Speak calmly through door → CALL 911 → call MEI: 719-344-2814
🤕 Someone is injured
CALL 911 — always.

Water — Shut Off Guide

Individual room · whole floor · whole building
▼

Individual Room Shut Off

  1. Find the ceiling access panel closest to the leak — open with a screwdriver.
  2. Use a ladder to locate and close the valve inside.
⚠️
If the leak continues after closing the valve, water is traveling down from a pipe above. Contact a manager immediately.

Whole Floor Shut Off

  1. Go to the housekeeping closet on the affected floor.
  2. Locate the water heater — find valves labeled "Hot Water Shut Off" and "Water Shut Off."
  3. Close the appropriate valve (hot only, cold only, or both).
  4. To drain remaining water from pipes: open sink faucets at both ends of the hallway until water stops running.
  5. Notify guests on the floor and contact a manager immediately.
Hot Water Shut Off (red tag) and Water Shut Off (blue tag) — in housekeeping closet on each floor

Whole Building Shut Off

🚨
Contact a manager BEFORE attempting this. This shuts off water for the entire property.
  1. Go to the hobbit closet near the elevator on the first floor.
  2. Locate the main water line to the right.
  3. Close the valve on the LEFT side (marked/tagged).
Main building water line — hobbit closet near elevator, 1st floor. Close the tagged valve on the LEFT.

Electric — Panel Guide

Breakers · room outages · full building outage
▼

Panel Key & Locations

  • Panel key is in the front desk safe, labeled "Electric Panels."
  • Each floor has two breaker boxes: one near the elevator (rooms 1–6) and one at the end of the hall (rooms 7–12).
  • Each panel has an interior directory showing which breaker corresponds to which room.

Reset a Tripped Breaker

  1. Locate the correct breaker box using the room number and floor (elevator = rooms 1–6, hallway end = rooms 7–12).
  2. Open with the panel key from the front desk safe.
  3. Find the breaker: ON = fully left. OFF = fully right. TRIPPED = stuck in the middle.
  4. To reset tripped breaker: flip it fully OFF first → wait 5 seconds → flip it ON.
  5. If problem persists: notify manager and consider relocating the guest.
Tripped breaker (stuck in middle). Flip fully OFF first, wait, then flip ON.
Normal breaker positions: fully left = ON, fully right = OFF.

Full Building Outage — While It's Out

  1. Call Black Hills Energy: 888-890-5554 — to check for a known utility outage in the area.
  2. Confirm no one is trapped in the elevator. (Elevator auto-returns to the nearest floor below on battery backup.)
  3. Pull a screenshot of in-house guest list from Cloudbeds.
  4. If outage lasts more than 10 minutes: message all in-house guests via Cloudbeds.
  5. If elevator issues arise: call MEI Dispatch: 719-344-2814.

Full Building Outage — After Power Restored

  • Wait 10 minutes before using the elevator.
  • Reignite all kitchen appliances (see Gas → Reignite Kitchen Appliances).
  • Walk each floor to confirm lights, HVAC, and room systems are back online.
  • Notify manager that restoration is complete.

Gas — Shut Off Guide

Fireplace · water heaters · kitchen appliances
▼
🚨
If you smell gas anywhere: Do NOT flip any switches. Evacuate the building and call 911. Then call Atmos Energy: 888-286-6700.

Fireplace (Lobby)

  1. Get a Phillips head screwdriver from the black/green toolbox in the hotel manager's office.
  2. Remove the vent panel.
  3. Turn the blue knob to cut the gas supply.
Lobby fireplace — arrow points to vent panel. Remove with Phillips screwdriver to access blue gas knob.
Blue gas shut-off knob inside the fireplace vent — turn to cut gas supply.

Water Heater (Basement + Floors 2, 3, 4)

  1. Locate the water heater with the issue.
  2. Find the yellow tag labeled "Gas Shut Off."
  3. Turn the handle until it stops.
Water heater gas shut-off — yellow tag reads "Water Heater Gas Shut Off." Turn handle until it stops.

Kitchen Appliance Shut Off

  • Gas handles are green, located behind each appliance.
  • Turn fully until the handle stops (handle perpendicular to the pipe = OFF).
Green gas handle behind the grill — turn until perpendicular to the pipe to shut off.

Reignite Kitchen Appliances (After Power Outage)

  1. Go to the prep line and find the screen on the ceiling hood vent.
  2. Press the top right button to open the gas lines to the kitchen.
  3. Confirm the green gas handle behind each machine is in the ON position (parallel to the pipe).
  4. Use a lighter to light the pilot on each appliance:
    • Grill: light each section and confirm all ignite.
    • Stovetop: pilots are under the grates — accessible from the front.
Hood vent screen on ceiling — press the top right button (GAS ON) to open gas lines to kitchen.
Grill pilot light lit (blue flame visible through slot). Confirm flame before moving to next section.
Stovetop pilot — blue flame visible through the hole under the grate. Lit and confirmed.
Internal gas assembly behind appliance — for reference if a pilot won't catch.

Plumbing — Procedures

Leaks · drain blockages · toilets
▼

Water Leak

  1. Identify the source and close the nearest pipe valve (see Water Shut Off Guide above).
  2. Notify manager.
  3. Relocate affected guests if necessary.

Drain Blockage — Sink or Shower

Snakes are in the hobbit closet near the elevator on the first floor. Small plastic snakes = sinks/showers.

  1. Remove the drain cover with a screwdriver.
  2. Insert the small plastic snake and work it through to dislodge the blockage.

Drain Blockage — Toilet

Toilet auger (long metal snake) stored in the trash bag in the basement at the office across the street — 110 N 7th, behind Standard Oil.

  1. Insert the auger as far as possible into the toilet drain.
  2. Spin the handle in circles to dislodge the blockage.
⚠️
Do NOT push the auger to the bottom if it extends far without resistance. An object may be lodged and allowing the snake to pass. Pushing to the bottom will break the toilet's wax seal. Stop and contact management.

HVAC — Procedures

Unit issues · guest comfort · when to call
▼

Individual Room Unit Not Working

  1. Check that the thermostat in the room is set correctly and the unit has power.
  2. Check the breaker for that room (see Electric panel guide).
  3. If still not working: notify manager and offer the guest a room change if available.

Building-Wide HVAC Issue

  1. Note which floors or areas are affected.
  2. Do NOT attempt to service central HVAC equipment — call Vision Mechanical.
  3. Weekdays: call 719-547-0218. After hours / emergencies: 719-696-1892.
  4. While waiting: open windows where possible for ventilation, notify manager, and update affected guests.
ℹ️
Do not attempt to service central HVAC units, boilers, or rooftop equipment. Vision Mechanical handles all repairs.

Elevator — Procedures

Person trapped · power outage · service call
▼

Person Trapped in Elevator

🚨
Call 911 immediately. Do not attempt to open the elevator doors manually.
  1. Speak calmly to the person through the door: "We know you're in there — help is on the way. Stay calm and stay away from the doors."
  2. Call 911.
  3. Call MEI Dispatch: 719-344-2814.
  4. Call elevator territory manager Billy Carroll: 719-505-2658 or billy.carroll@meiusa.com
  5. Notify manager immediately.
  6. Block elevator doors on all floors so no one else attempts to use it.

Elevator During Power Outage

  • The elevator automatically returns to the nearest floor below on battery backup and opens its doors.
  • Confirm the elevator is empty and no one is trapped.
  • Do not use the elevator until power has been restored for at least 10 minutes.
  • If elevator fails to return automatically: call MEI Dispatch 719-344-2814 immediately.

Non-Emergency Service

Call MEI: 719-344-2814 or contact Billy Carroll at billy.carroll@meiusa.com.

Evacuation Protocol

Assembly point · guest & staff headcount
▼
🚨
Evacuate FIRST. Account for everyone SECOND. Do not delay evacuation to gather belongings or check systems.
Step 1

Call 911. Give the address: 631 Main St, Cañon City, CO 81212.

Step 2

Activate fire alarm if not already triggered. Alert all floors verbally as you evacuate.

Step 3

Assembly point: Standard Oil — 110 N 7th St, directly behind the hotel.

Step 4

Pull the in-house guest list from Cloudbeds on your phone and run a headcount at Standard Oil.

Step 5

Pull the staff-on-duty list from Sling and confirm all staff are accounted for.

Step 6

Report headcount status to 911 dispatcher and on-call manager. Do not re-enter until cleared by fire department.

📍
Assembly point: Standard Oil — 110 N 7th St (behind the hotel). Keep everyone together and away from the building.
