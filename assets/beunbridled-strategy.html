The Core Insight

Two very different products are being built right now:

  • Enterprise AI — unlocking firewalls, navigating IT, complex org networks. Infinitely harder.
  • Small Business Digital Chief of Staff — off-grid, fast to deploy, immediate ROI. More marketable.

The latter wins. Build that first.

The Window

This has a potential to expire. First-mover advantage in the small business AI chief of staff space is real — but not permanent. The intake process, skill-building, and automatic deployment pipeline have to be a now thing.

The Missing Layer: Personal AI for Team Members

This is distinct from micro-apps and enterprise infrastructure — and arguably the most important unlock. The vision: your team members' AI checks their email every day, drafts responses, creates documents, replies on their behalf. They type less, talk more, do what they're good at.

That's not a web app you visit. That's an always-on digital chief of staff for each person. It requires change management, beta testing, and security sign-off — and it's going to take longer than it should. That's the honest reality.

The Three Hats

Stan sees AI through three different lenses — each with a different role, opinion, and stake.

Hat 1 — Entrepreneurial (BeUnbridled)

Build something specialized, defensible, recurring. Specialize in 2–3 verticals. Say no to enterprise complexity. Protect the off-grid advantage. Get to 15 clients at $3,500/mo, then scale.

Hat 2 — Unbridled (Internal)

AI adoption can't live in IT alone — it has to live in the operators and solution architects. The bottleneck model fails. AI needs a maker inside, not just an IT gatekeeper. Stan's role: unlock and unkink, not own. Everyone needs to understand the value and be pulling for it.

Hat 3 — Sol4 (Advisory)

Push Sol4 to be solutions-oriented, agile, entrepreneurial about AI — not just managing infrastructure. AI as a service to enterprise clients. The hackathon showed the problem clearly: firewall after firewall, access denied, brutal to navigate. Organizations are slow to adopt AI precisely because of permissioning complexity — that's the problem Sol4 needs to be solving, not just documenting.

Where the time goes

Of the three hats, the middle one (Unbridled internal AI) is where Stan believes his time goes right now — but BeUnbridled is where the entrepreneurial energy and cap table opportunity live. The goal is to free up enough time to run both.

The Builder Question

The core tension: does Unbridled need a dedicated maker inside the org, or does BeUnbridled function as a shared service bureau that bills back?

The case for inside: Too important to be fractional. If whoever's building AI is also fielding restaurant client requests, they're distracted by definition. You need someone whose sole focus is making and moving for the sake of Unbridled.

The case for outside (BeUnbridled as shared service): Every time Unbridled has tried to build capability internally — apps, gamification, web dev — it got slow and eventually died. Outside the org: agility, IP compounds, no consensus drag, no competing priorities.

"We kill everything." — the honest track record

Where it landed: BeUnbridled stays outside as the incubator. Bills back to Unbridled on retainer. Justin gets a clean mandate. Solutions gets a dedicated resource eventually — it just comes from BeUnbridled headcount as we scale, not a Solutions hire. Pivot to an inside hire if and when the product naturally evolves that direction.
