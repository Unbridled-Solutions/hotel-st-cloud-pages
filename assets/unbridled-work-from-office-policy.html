Together We're Better

Our approach to where we work, why it matters, and what we're protecting when we show up together.

Unbridled was never built in a conference room. It was built in the in-between moments — the hallway conversation that became a strategy, the lunch that became a partnership, the Friday afternoon that became someone's turning point. That doesn't happen on a Zoom call.

We believe in presence. Not because we don't trust our people to work from home — we do. But because what we're building here isn't just a company. It's an ecosystem. And ecosystems don't thrive in isolation. They thrive through proximity, through collision, through the kind of spontaneous connection you can't schedule.

This policy isn't about clocking in. It's about protecting something we've worked hard to build — a culture where people know each other, trust each other, and actually enjoy doing this work together. That culture is a competitive advantage. And like all competitive advantages, it requires investment to maintain.

"The ceiling of a leader who builds leaders is generational. But leaders only build leaders when they're in the room together."

Stan Bullis · Founder, Unbridled

Why Presence Matters

We've watched what happens when teams go fully remote. The work gets done — usually. But the culture quietly hollows out. The new team member who needed a mentor doesn't get one. The cross-department idea that would have changed everything never gets sparked. The person who was struggling doesn't get seen until it's too late.

In-person isn't about oversight. It's about opportunity. It's about creating the conditions for the kind of collaboration, mentorship, and serendipity that only happens when people are physically in the same space. Research consistently shows that in-person teams make faster decisions, develop stronger relationships, onboard more effectively, and innovate more freely. We're not going against the grain here — we're reading the data.

And at Unbridled, we have something most companies don't: a physical environment that was intentionally designed to inspire. You work in a building that's been deliberately crafted to unlock creativity, connection, and performance. That space works for you — but only if you show up.

What the Best Companies Are Doing

We're not alone in this conviction. Some of the most respected organizations in the world have drawn the same conclusion — and said it out loud.

Apple
3 Days In-Office Required
Tim Cook made the case plainly: "We believe that in-person collaboration is essential to our culture and our future." Apple requires Monday, Tuesday, and Thursday in office — calling out the specific value of in-person collaboration for innovation and mentorship. They believe the best ideas happen in the hallways, not on Slack.
Amazon
5 Days In-Office (2025)
Andy Jassy wrote to employees: "We've observed that it's easier for our teammates to learn, model, practice, and strengthen our culture when we're in the office together." Amazon moved to five days in 2025, specifically citing culture strength, collaboration speed, and the ability to mentor junior team members as primary drivers.
JPMorgan Chase
5 Days In-Office (Senior Leadership)
Jamie Dimon was blunt: "Remote work doesn't work for spontaneous idea generation, for management of people, or — most importantly — for culture." The bank moved senior leaders to full in-office, citing the irreplaceable value of real-time relationship-building and the kind of trust that only forms through sustained in-person contact.
Goldman Sachs
5 Days In-Office
David Solomon called remote work "an aberration that we're going to correct as quickly as possible." Goldman's return-to-office was rooted in one conviction: a culture of excellence requires mentorship, and mentorship requires proximity. You cannot apprentice someone you've never been in a room with.

We're not asking for five days. We're asking for four. We think that's the right balance — enough presence to maintain a thriving culture, enough flexibility to honor the reality of modern life.

Our Policy

The Unbridled Solutions Work Policy

4 days in office. 1 day flexible. That's the requirement. In a standard work week, Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday are mandatory in-office anchor days for the entire team — no exceptions. The fourth in-office day is yours to choose: Monday or Friday, but one of them must be in office every week. The remaining day is fully flexible — work from home, a coffee shop, wherever you do your best thinking.

To be direct: four days in office is the mandate. Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday are fixed. Your fourth day is your choice — Monday or Friday. The fifth day is your own.

What We're Protecting

This isn't a policy about distrust. It's a policy about intentionality. Here's what we're protecting when we ask you to show up:

The Culture

Culture is not a document. It's not a slide deck or a set of values painted on a wall. Culture is what happens in real time, between real people, in real spaces. It lives in the conversation after the meeting, the encouragement in the hallway, the moment someone watches how a leader handles a hard situation. You can't transmit that over video. You can only live it.

Your Growth

The people around you are your greatest professional development resource. The mentor who changes your trajectory isn't going to find you on a Teams call — they're going to notice you across the room, pull up a chair, and ask what you're working on. That only happens when you're here.

The Ecosystem

Unbridled is 32 companies. Some of the most valuable work that happens inside our walls isn't within your team — it's across them. The real estate insight that helps the live events team, the hospitality approach that shapes the construction culture — these cross-pollinations are the engine of what makes us different. They require presence.

Each Other

We are a community before we are a company. We've always believed that the people we work with are not a means to an end — they are the point. That belief requires investment. Showing up is the most basic form of investment there is.

Destiny is the input. Doing Good by Doing Well is the output. But neither happens in isolation — they happen in collaboration, in community, in rooms full of people who are tuned in to each other and to what's possible.

A Note From Stan

I've been building companies for 25 years. I've watched great cultures thrive and I've watched great cultures quietly fade. The difference was almost never strategy or resources. It was almost always proximity.

When I interview people for Unbridled — or when I used to, before the team grew past the point where I could interview everyone — I wasn't looking for the most technically qualified candidate. I was looking for the person who wanted to be here. Who was drawn to something bigger than a job title. Who believed that the way we do business can change the world.

Those people are worth protecting. And the best way to protect them — and to keep drawing more of them to us — is to build a place that's worth showing up to. A place where presence is reciprocated, where effort is visible, where who you are matters as much as what you produce.

That's what we're asking for. Show up. Be present. Let the place work on you — and let the people around you work on you too. That's where the best version of Unbridled lives.

— Stan Bullis, Founder · Unbridled Solutions

Questions & Exceptions

Life happens. We know that. If you have a specific circumstance that requires a temporary adjustment to this schedule — medical, family, travel — talk to your manager. We handle these things like adults, with grace and good judgment. What we ask is that exceptions are exceptions, not the default.

If your role is structured differently (remote-first, field-based, or travel-heavy), your manager will clarify the specific expectations that apply to you. The spirit of this policy — presence, connection, investment in each other — applies universally, even when the specifics vary.
