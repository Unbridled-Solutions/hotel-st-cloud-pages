Hub + Spoke + Wheel: does this structure match the model?

✓ Where it supports the ecosystem model

  • This IS the Hub + Spoke + Wheel. Allison at center. Four spokes. Each spoke's team is a wheel — autonomous, specialized, capable of expanding without touching the others.
  • Authority is distributed, not concentrated. Three Creative Directors each own a full creative wheel. Danielle owns ops. Nobody needs to ask the hub for routine creative decisions.
  • Ops is structurally separated from creative. Danielle's wheel protects creative flow from administrative drag. That's Credibility cornerstone in action.
  • New roles build capability, not hierarchy. Brandon → Video CD, CJ → Technical Design, Kate → Ops Support. More wheels. No new management layers. Pure ecosystem thinking.
  • Career paths are visible. The GD who trains with CJ before moving to a CD's wheel is an "Unusually Good Career" made tangible.

△ Watch closely

  • 65% reporting changes is a human moment, not just a structural one. 15 of 23 people change their spoke. The org chart is right — the comms plan is what makes this land as a call forward, not a reshuffling.
  • Jil Cave is on two spokes simultaneously. Ty's wheel until 8/1, then moving. Clean, communicated transition plan is essential — nobody performs well in ambiguous orbit.
  • Three open Presentation Designer positions = a concentration of risk. Until those wheels are staffed, capacity planning must account for the gap explicitly.

Verdict

  • The structure is sound and genuinely reflects the ecosystem model from the culture deck. The visual shift from hierarchy to hub-and-spoke isn't just aesthetic — it's a leadership signal. Show people this chart, not the tree. It tells a different story about who they are and how they work.
