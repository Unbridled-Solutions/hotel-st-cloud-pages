Cleaning Economics
Why Cleaning Is the Biggest Variable
Assumption Value
Key insight: At a cabin this size (12,000 sf, sleeps 22), the cleaning cost gap vs. cleaning fee charged can be significant. If you charge guests $2,500 but the real cost is $5,200 per turn, you're absorbing $2,700 per stay. Minimize this by: (1) charging a higher cleaning fee or including it in the nightly rate, (2) building in-house linen laundry on-site, (3) extending minimum stays to reduce turn frequency.
