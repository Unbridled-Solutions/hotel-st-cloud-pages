🏔️ What Drives Cost in Grand County

💧
Well & Septic — Your Biggest Wildcard
These two line items alone can swing $40K–$90K depending on geology. Grand County wells routinely go 400–600 feet through granite. Get a well log from a neighbor before you budget.
🪵
Log Package Quality
Full-round custom logs vs. milled half-log siding are worlds apart. True full-round log construction is more expensive but is what gives a mountain cabin its look and thermal mass.
👷
Mountain Labor Premium
Grand County labor runs 20–30% above Denver metro. Trades drive long distances, charge accordingly, and are often booked 6–12 months out. Lock subs early.
🍳
Kitchen is the Biggest Finish Swing
A nice kitchen can be $25K or $80K depending on how custom you go. This is where the "moderate to upscale" spread is widest. Cabinet brand and stone selection are the two biggest levers.
📐
Settling Allowances Add Cost
Log cabins settle 3–6 inches in the first 2 years. Every penetration — mechanical, window, stair — needs a settling space built in and revisited at year 1–2. Factor in a revisit budget.
❄️
Snow Engineering & Ice Management
100+ PSF structural stamp, ice + water shield, proper roof pitch for shedding — these are not optional in Grand County. They cost more upfront and save thousands in repairs.

💡 Where to Spend and Where to Save

Spend on the roof and windows — they protect everything below them. Standing seam metal and energy-rated glazing are worth every dollar in Grand County winters.
Spend on radiant heat — in a log cabin at altitude, this is the right system. Forced air dries out logs and the interior. Radiant is worth the premium.
Spend on the kitchen — it's the heart of a mountain cabin. Nice stone, solid wood cabinets. You will not regret it.
Save on landscaping — at Grand County elevations, native seeding and minimal grading is the right move anyway. Don't over-engineer it.
Save on square footage — a tight, well-finished 1,000 sqft cabin beats a sprawling 1,800 sqft cabin with builder-grade finishes every time.
Don't skip contingency — budget 10% and do not touch it until you're in drywall. Mountain builds always find something. Always.
Lock in your GC and key subs before you break ground — don't shop price at the last minute. Mountain trades are scarce and book out fast.
Get the well log before you finalize your budget — one well blowing past 500 feet can add $20K–$40K. Know your geology first.
