A Personal Letter

To Phillina Morgan

From Stan Bullis  ·  July 2026

Phillina,

We talked the other day, and I meant every word. But I wanted to put it in writing, to reiterate my heart for you, where I stand on this transition, and to bring some real clarity to a new role I want to create. One I think you're the perfect person for.

Twenty years. That's not a tenure. That's a life built inside something together.

I've watched you grow from a meeting planner into a Senior Vice President and partner. I've watched you help build Unbridled Travel, GIFT'D, Unbridled Productions, Unbridled Media, and now Orange Door from the inside out. I've watched you navigate the 2009 crisis, COVID, the great resignation, and every hard season in between without ever losing your character, your candor, or your care for the people around you.

In 20 years, I cannot think of a single moment where you were anything less than exactly who you are. Not one. That is extraordinarily rare, and it says everything.

You call people higher. You build teams that hold. You make smart decisions under pressure and with grace. You are, without question, one of the finest people I have ever had the privilege of leading alongside.

So when you told me you were thinking about stepping away, I didn't hear someone quitting. I heard someone who had given everything, who had a promise she made to herself and to Ella and Henry, and who was finally ready to honor it.

I respect that completely.

But I also know that losing you from this ecosystem entirely would be one of the greatest losses Unbridled has ever experienced. And I don't think it has to be that way.

A New Chapter

The Opportunity

Unbridled Companies — The Family Office, Rebuilt

What I want to offer you isn't a consolation prize. It's something we've done before for people who've earned a different kind of relationship with this company, people like Jen Bing and Diane Martin, where we build a role around someone's life instead of the other way around.

About ten years ago, I had a vision for Unbridled Companies to function as a true family office — a central hub that stewarded a growing portfolio of businesses, identified new opportunities, and made sure we were living out our mission across every company we touch. That vision never fully materialized. Unbridled Solutions ended up carrying most of that weight.

I'm ready to change that now. And I want you to help me build it.

The goal is to reestablish Unbridled Companies as the family office it was always meant to be, one that stewards our current portfolio of 32 businesses and grows that to 100, all in service of doing good by doing well.

Here's what makes this role profound. A family office that works alongside growing businesses and young entrepreneurs needs people with genuine credibility. People who have actually lived through the highs and the lows, the wins and the losses, the cycles that every small business goes through. You and Lindsay are two of the most credible people I know for this work, because you haven't just witnessed those cycles. You've lived them, in real time, inside real companies. That's rare. That's exactly what our entrepreneurs need alongside them.

Our 32 companies span five market segments. Your role touches all of them.

Live Events

Our most profitable companies — the ones you helped build. You remain a partner in Unbridled Solutions. But your day-to-day relationship shifts, and the travel stops.

Real Estate

15 properties, most of them historic buildings. The family office manages leases, insurance, property tax, maintenance, and long-term place-making strategy.

Hospitality

Hotel St. Cloud, Fremont Public House, Fremont Provisions, Fremont Makers. Your perspective from hotels around the world, plus strategic advisory for the 2029 New Market Tax Credit restructuring.

Portfolio Companies

Unbridled Contractors, Unbridled Wealth, Unbridled Insurance, Artist Uprising, and others. These companies need a strategic home and a conduit into shared services. The family office becomes that.

Mission

Unbridled Companies and UnbridledACTS are inseparable. A meaningful role in our charitable work, and the chance to bring our managing partners together annually to share stories and build each other up.

Your Specific Role

You'd work alongside Lindsay, our Chief of Staff. You two already respect each other, already trust each other. That relationship is already built.

On the Unbridled Solutions side, I want you to lead our AI modernization effort. What we're building with BeUnbridled AI is exactly what Unbridled Solutions needs — smarter event registration, planning, contracts, logistics. You've held every position in that company. Nobody understands how those workflows actually run better than you do. You're the perfect person to guide that transformation from the outside, without the weight of running it.

On the Unbridled Companies side, we're building a shared services accounting team for all non-live-event companies. That includes hiring a CFO, establishing financial rhythms, P&L accountability, cash flow analysis, and balance sheets across the portfolio. The infrastructure is already partially in place. This is a clean migration, not a build from scratch. We're also adding two new companies in August: Fremont Makers and BeUnbridled AI.

Your Life

There is no travel requirement. There are no long weekends. There is no office you're required to sit in.

Every stakeholder in this role is internal. The nature of the work is strategic, not operational and on-the-road. You work when you want, from where you want, and how you want.

And I know you. I suspect you'll want an office. So let's find you one. Whether that's the Carriage House, the Productions space, or the third floor of the Grand Street Mansion with me, we'll make sure you have a real home base that fits.

Ella's games are on the calendar. Henry's years are yours to show up for. That's not a side effect of this role. That's the whole point.

You've given this company everything. You've been a model of character, grit, and grace for two decades. Now I want to offer you something that matches the life you've earned.

I hope you say yes.

With deep gratitude,

Stan Bullis

Founder, Unbridled Companies
