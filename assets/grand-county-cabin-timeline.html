⚠️ Grand County — High-Country Watch Items

❄️
Snow Load Engineering100+ PSF roof design load at elevation. Structural stamp is not optional — Grand County will require it.
🏔️
Frost Depth36–48 inches minimum depending on zone. Foundation depth is everything up here — don't cut corners.
🔥
Fire MitigationGrand County has defensible space rules. Log exteriors need fire-rated stain. Budget and timeline for it.
📐
Log SettlingGreen logs settle 3–6 inches over 2 years. Every window, door, stair, and mech penetration must have a settling space built in.
📋
Permit TimelineGrand County is not fast. Submit Day 1 of Month 1, not Month 2. Build 6–8 weeks of permit wait into your schedule.
🌡️
Weather WindowsConcrete: May–Sep. Roofing: Jun–Oct. Start concrete in spring or you'll be fighting freezes and cost overruns.
🪵
Log Lead TimesLog package manufacturers run 8–16 weeks. Order in Month 2 or your shell crew will be sitting idle in Month 5.
💧
Well & SepticBoth require separate state permits in Grand County. Budget 6–10 weeks just for those approvals. Do not treat as an afterthought.
Phase Details

What Happens When

Phase 1 — Pre-Construction

Months 1–3
Engage architect, lock GC and key subs
Site survey, soil test, geotechnical report
Submit for Grand County building permit
Structural engineer stamp (snow load critical)
Well + septic design and CDPHE permit
Place log package order (8–16 wk lead time)
Finalize bid packages with all subs

Phase 2 — Site & Foundation

Months 3–5
Clear and grade building pad
Excavate to 48" frost depth
Install drainage and gravel bed
Form and pour concrete — target May pour
Waterproofing and exterior insulation board
Backfill, compact, and set anchor bolts for sill log

Phase 3 — Log Shell & Roof

Months 5–8
Log package delivered — crane / boom truck access
Sill logs set, sealed, and anchored
Wall logs stacked course by course
Window and door bucks with settling spaces
Ridge beam, rafters, and gable ends
Roof deck, ice + water shield (required)
Dark charcoal standing seam metal roofing

Phase 4 — Rough Mechanicals

Months 8–10
Pass rough framing inspection before closing walls
Rough electrical throughout
Rough plumbing — supply, DWV
Radiant heat rough-in (recommended for log cabin)
Propane line rough-in
Exterior windows and doors hung
Chinking and exterior log stain (coat 1)

Phase 5 — Insulation / Drywall

Months 10–11
Spray foam at rim joists and penetrations
Batts at any non-log partition walls
Vapor barrier details
Drywall or T&G pine throughout
Pass insulation and framing final inspections

Phase 6 — Interior Finishes

Months 11–15
T&G pine or hardwood flooring rough install
Cabinet delivery and installation
Stone countertop template + 3–4 wk fabrication
Fireplace / wood stove + chimney
Plumbing trim-out — fixtures and sinks
Electrical trim-out — panel final + fixtures
HVAC commissioning
Paint, finish floors, hardware, and accessories

Phase 7 — Site Finish & Punch

Months 15–18
Deck, porch framing, decking, and stairs
Exterior railings and guardrails
Exterior log stain — second coat
Septic final inspection
Driveway gravel and final grading
Native landscaping — minimal at altitude
Final building inspection — Certificate of Occupancy
Punch list walk + deep clean — move-in ready 🏔️
